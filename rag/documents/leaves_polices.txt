TITLE: Global Employee Leave & Time-Off Policy

DOCUMENT_TYPE: Policy
DEPARTMENT: Human Resources
LAST_UPDATED: 15 January 2025
VERSION: 3.2

SUMMARY:
This comprehensive policy defines employee leave entitlements, eligibility, approval workflows, compliance requirements, and exceptions across all business units globally. It ensures consistency, fairness, and adherence to labor laws.

KEYWORDS:
leave policy, PTO, sick leave, casual leave, maternity leave, HR compliance, time off

CONTENT:

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a structured and transparent framework for managing employee leave across the organization. It ensures compliance with statutory regulations while maintaining operational efficiency.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time employees across all geographies unless overridden by local labor laws or contractual agreements.

## 3. Types of Leave

### 3.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)
- Employees are entitled to 20 working days annually.
- PTO accrues monthly at a prorated basis.
- PTO can be used for personal, vacation, or emergency purposes.

### 3.2 Sick Leave
- 10 days per year allocated.
- Medical certificate required for absence exceeding 2 consecutive days.
- Extended sick leave subject to HR approval.

### 3.3 Casual Leave
- 6 days annually.
- Typically used for short-term personal needs.

### 3.4 Maternity Leave
- 26 weeks of paid leave as per statutory guidelines.
- Additional unpaid leave may be granted on case-by-case basis.

### 3.5 Paternity Leave
- 5 working days.
- Must be availed within 3 months of childbirth.

### 3.6 Unpaid Leave (Loss of Pay)
- Applicable when leave balance is exhausted.
- Requires manager and HR approval.

## 4. Leave Application Process
- All leave requests must be submitted via HRMS.
- Manager approval is mandatory before leave commencement.
- HR approval required for long-term leave (>10 days).

## 5. Carry Forward Rules
- Maximum 10 days PTO can be carried forward.
- Carry-forward leaves expire after 6 months.

## 6. Encashment Policy
- Employees can encash unused PTO above 10 days annually.
- Encashment rates based on basic salary.

## 7. Compliance & Audit
- HR conducts quarterly audits of leave records.
- Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action.

## 8. Exceptions & Special Cases
- Emergency leave can be regularized post approval.
- Compassionate leave handled on a case-by-case basis.
