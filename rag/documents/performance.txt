TITLE: Performance Management & Appraisal Framework

DOCUMENT_TYPE: Policy
DEPARTMENT: Human Resources
LAST_UPDATED: 01 February 2025
VERSION: 4.1

SUMMARY:
This document defines the organization’s performance management framework, including evaluation criteria, rating methodology, calibration processes, and linkage to compensation and promotions.

KEYWORDS:
performance review, appraisal, KPI, employee evaluation, promotion, rating system

CONTENT:

## 1. Objective
To create a fair, transparent, and performance-driven culture aligned with business goals.

## 2. Performance Rating Scale
- 5: Outstanding Performer
- 4: Exceeds Expectations
- 3: Meets Expectations
- 2: Needs Improvement
- 1: Unsatisfactory

## 3. Key Evaluation Parameters
- Business Impact & Results
- Quality of Deliverables
- Innovation and Problem Solving
- Team Collaboration & Leadership
- Communication Skills
- Adherence to Deadlines

## 4. Performance Cycle
- Mid-Year Review: June
- Annual Review: December

## 5. Goal Setting
- Goals must be SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Defined at the beginning of the performance cycle

## 6. Calibration Process
- Cross-functional calibration ensures fairness
- Conducted by leadership panel
- Adjustments made to avoid rating bias

## 7. Compensation Linkage
- Ratings directly impact salary increments
- Bonus eligibility tied to performance rating

## 8. Promotion Criteria
- Consistent rating of 4 or above
- Leadership potential
- Business requirement

## 9. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Employees rated 1 or 2 may be placed on PIP
- Duration: 60–90 days
- Continuous monitoring and feedback
